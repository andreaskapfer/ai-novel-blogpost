**Prose Style Directive for "Wo die Schielen enden"**

---

**1. Sentence Rhythm Patterns**  
* **Temporal Pacing:** Mirror historical epochs through cadence:  
  - **1903–1918 (Act 1):** Fluid, compound sentences echoing agricultural rhythms ("The locomotive’s whistle unfurled across barley fields, a serpentine sound that coiled around church steeples and clung to the thatched eaves of barns").  
  - **1919–1933 (Act 2):** Disjointed fragments amid longer condemnations of inflation ("Paper marks fluttered like dead leaves. Worthless. A goat now cost sixty thousand").  
  - **1934–1945 (Act 3):** Machine-gun staccato for violence; choked, protracted syntax for moral dilemmas ("Karl gripped the throttle. The cattle cars waited. Beyond the platform—smoke, always smoke").  

* **Action vs. Reflection:**  
  - Battle/deportation scenes: Austere Hauptsatz constructions ("Shells screamed. Glass shattered. Franz crouched beneath the timetable board").  
  - Introspection: Subordinate clauses weaving memory and guilt ("Elisabeth traced the rail map’s arteries, wondering when the tracks had become veins draining the village’s blood").  

---

**2. Paragraph Architecture**  
* **Density Gradient:**  
  - Open with thick sensory blocks establishing locale ("Fog pooled in the Lech Valley, smothering wheat stalks pregnant with summer dew, when the first rail crews arrived bearing picks that glittered like alien tools").  
  - Compress during crises: Single-sentence paragraphs for traumatic reveals ("Heinrich never received Emil’s body").  

* **Focal Transitions:**  
  - Shift perspective via juxtaposed micro-paragraphs:  
    *Franz filed the deportation orders.*  
    *Three streets away, Elisabeth burned her childhood lace curtains to warm David’s hands.*  

---

**3. Descriptive Doctrine**  
* **Tactile Historicism:**  
  - **Industrial vs. Agrarian Textures:** Contrast "locomotive grease black as spoiled marrow" with "peat-rich soil crumbling like dark cake."  
  - **Era-Specific Nuances:** Hyperinflation sequence highlights sensory decay ("potatoes reeking of cellar rot were banknotes now").  

* **Symbolic Recurrence:**  
  - Rail infrastructure evolves: iron sprouts (1903) → rusted fetters (1933) → twisted skeletons (1945).  
  - Assign leitmotifs: Heinrich smells turned earth; Franz detects coal tar; Karl tastes bile during transports.  

---

**4. Metaphor and Motif Protocols**  
* **Railway as Central Metaphor:**  
  - Tracks = destiny ("Schienen des Schicksals"), diverging points = moral choices ("Elisabeth stood where the sidings branched—a breath, then she pushed the lever west").  
  - Trains as perverted progress: Pre-war "iron stallions" become "metal coffins" under NS regime.  

* **Frequency Rules:**  
  - Deploy metaphors sparingly at plot hinges (one per major decision/atrocity).  
  - **Forbidden:** Clichéd comparisons ("war’s storm," "Nazi cancer"). Opt for era-specific imagery:  
  → NS rise: "The party spread through the village like blight on rye."  
  → Bombing aftermath: "Ash fell like counterfeit snow, greasing the river where salmon once leapt."  

---

**5. Tense/POV Conventions**  
* **Third-Limited Discipline:**  
  - **Heinrich Chapters (1903–1930):** Pastoral lexicon; free indirect speech mimicking Bavarian cadence ("Ach, these youngsters craving locomotive stink over good manure").  
  - **Elisabeth’s POV (1925–1945):** Tight focalization—omit Nazi terminology she wouldn’t use ("the brownshirts," never "SA").  
  - **Karl’s Segments:** Stream-of-consciousness during desertion ("left right left the tracks keep time with my pulse don’t look back").  

* **Tense Integrity:**  
  - Strict past tense, excluding character memories rendered in present ("The stationmaster’s whistle shrills again in Franz’s dreams").  

---

**6. Narrative Voice Parameters**  
* **Historical Voice:**  
  - **1900s:** Omniscient traces ("None yet knew these rails would carry more than grain"), phasing into claustrophobic limited POV post-1918.  
* **Tone Matrix:**  
  - **Pre-1914:** Nuanced irony ("Progress, declared the burgomaster, as cows bolted from the screaming locomotive").  
  - **War Years:** Unadorned reportage ("Winter 1917: Turnips. Only turnips").  
  - **NS Era:** Disassociated passives ("Mistakes were corrected. Undesirables were removed") until moral ruptures demand active voice ("Franz shot the SS guard").  

* **Dialog vs. Narration:**  
  - Bavarian dialect sparingly in early acts ("Grüß Gott" → sterile Hochdeutsch after 1933).  
  - Inner monologues italicized; regime euphemisms in quotes ("resettlement actions").  

---

**7. Structural Resonance**  
* **Chapter Cadence:**  
  - Open/close chapters with railway sounds: hopeful whistles (Ch. 1) → air-raid sirens (Ch. 12).  
* **Thematic Callbacks:**  
  - Heinrich’s 1903 protest speech echoes in Karl’s 1944 desertion ("You said they’d chain us to progress, Großvater. You were kind").  

---

**Directive Intent:** Forge prose that *embodies* technological oppression—language itself should feel welded, riveted, then shattered alongside the characters. The railway’s inexorable momentum must thrum beneath every sentence until silence falls in the final ashes.
