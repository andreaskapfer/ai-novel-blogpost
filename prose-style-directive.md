### **PROSE STYLE DIRECTIVE: WO DIE SCHIENEN ENDEN**

#### **NARRATIVE VOICE**  
- **Tone:** Lyrical yet restrained, balancing historical gravitas with intimate character psychology. Voice darkens progressively from pastoral nostalgia (Part I) to grim pragmatism (Part III).  
- **Perspective:** Close third-person limited, adhering to one viewpoint per chapter. Sensory immersion prioritizes the focal character’s *physical experiences* (e.g., Otto smelling dynamite-churned earth, Maria hearing frozen wheels screech on wartime tracks).  
- **Authority:** Embed historical authenticity via precise technical vocabulary (*Schwellenholz*, *Stellwerk*, *Verschiebebahnhof*) and period-specific idioms, avoiding overt exposition.  

---

#### **SENTENCE RHYTHM**  
1. **Slow passages (tradition/reflection):**  
   - Compound sentences with semicolons, emulating rail tracks’ parallel lines:  
     *"The Marienquelle’s waters still bubbled, thick with limestone; Otto watched them seep into dynamite cracks, his knuckles white around a scythe handle."*  
2. **Urgent passages (conflict/action):**  
   - Staccato fragments mimicking locomotive pistons:  
     *"Screech of metal. Sparks. Frantic hands wrenching bolts. Too late."*  
3. **Transitional scenes:**  
   - Anaphora for departures/arrivals:  
     *"The train took their sons. The train brought back ghosts. The train would demand more."*  

---

#### **PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE**  
| **Scene Type**       | **Structure**                                  | **Example Purpose**                          |  
|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|  
| **Descriptive**       | 4-6 sentences; tactile → emotional layers    | Introduce bombed-out Bahnhofshalle (Ch. 18) |  
| **Dialogue-heavy**    | Short alternating grafs; minimal tags         | Franz/Jakob confrontation (Ch. 12)          |  
| **Climactic**         | Single-line grafs isolating images            | Zugunglück body reveal (Ch. 16)             |  
| **Reflective**        | Meandering Whitmanesque lists                 | Cataloging war refugees’ belongings (Ch.18) |  

---

#### **DESCRIPTIVE DENSITY**  
- **Setting:** Three concrete details per key location (e.g., Bahnsteig: *tar-scented wind, pigeon droppings crusting benches, timetable posters curling at the edges*).  
- **Characters:** Focus on hands/eyes as emotional vectors. Lena’s calloused palms; Jakob’s *"hooded gaze that measured distances like cattle prices."*  
- **Eras:**  
  - **1900s:** Botanical precision (wildflower species, oak grain)  
  - **1920s-30s:** Industrial debris (coal dust, shrapnel-pocked steel)  
  - **1940s:** Synesthetic horror (*"deportation trains stank of rust and despair"*)  

---

#### **METAPHOR & SYMBOLISM**  
- **Frequency:** 1-2 sustained metaphors per chapter, evolving with the railway’s role:  
  - Part I: *"Gleise waren Adern, die das Dorfblut pumpen"* (life-giving veins)  
  - Part III: *"Schienen durchschnitten Landschaften wie Narben"* (wounds)  
- **Key Motifs:**  
  - **Clocks:** Bahnhofsuhr hands as societal turning points (e.g., *"The station clock’s minute hand twitched: one click closer to mobilization."*)  
  - **Water:** Contaminated springs → frozen tracks → blood thawing snow  

---

#### **TENSE & TEMPORALITY**  
- **Primary:** Deep past tense with intermittent *historical present* for traumatic flashbacks (e.g., Franz’s war memories).  
- **Temporal Markers:** Use train schedules/seasonal labor cycles to imply time’s passage:  
  *"Three harvests passed before the express trains came."*  

---

#### **SPECIAL CONVENTIONS**  
1. **Soundscapes:** Onomatopoeic integration of railway acoustics—*zischendem Dampf* (hissing steam) during tense scenes; silence when technology fails.  
2. **Documentary Interludes:** Reproduce ephemera (deportation lists, NSDAP pamphlets) in italics to fracture narrative flow.  
3. **Dialect:** Brief Swabian/Fränkisch phrases for elders’ dialogue (*"Des isch kei Fortschritt, des isch Wahnsinn!"*), fading as generations modernize.

### **SUMMARY**  
Craft prose that *embodies the railway’s rhythm*: lyrical momentum punctuated by violent halts. Balance meticulously researched technicity (track gauges, signaling protocols) with visceral human moments. Let sentences swell and contract like steam pressure—controlled until they explode.  

**Final Guiding Principle:**  
*"The rails are always present, even when unmentioned—a latent tension beneath every word."*
